Damnoen Saduak Floating Market

Photo Credit: Emad Aljumah/Jetty Images

Damnoen Saduak Floating Market is one of Thailand’s largest and most famous floating markets. It is a unique and breathtaking experience for tourists to get on a boat and buy local food and items such as kitchenware to clothing.

The Grand Palace

Photo Credit: Getty Images

The Grand Palace is considered the heart of Thailand. It is a historically significant place, and the place that houses the Emerald Buddha. It was the official residence of Thai kings for centuries. This is one of the most sacred spots in Thailand, and a must-visit location. Just remember-- to show respect, make sure to dress modestly!

Khao Yai National Park

Photo Credit: Nomadic Matt

Khao Yai National Park is a natural tourist attraction. Spanning more than 2,000 square kilometers of forest and grassland and 50 km of hiking and biking trails, this park is a must-visit destination when you go to Bangkok. It has a diverse set of wildlife and is a wonderful place to visit for nature enthusiasts.
