Photo Credit: Michael Breitung Photography

Between the 8th-10th century, the Thai people migrated from southern China in mass, establishing powerful sovereigns influenced by Indian culture. In Thailand’s making, many kingdoms rose and fell, like the Sukhothai and the Aytthaya Kingdoms. It is only in 1782 after the fall of Ayutthaya that Bangkok was established and the first king was crowned.

Before You Go

  • Siam was officially renamed to Thailand in 1939. This change in name reflects the country’s desire for freedom as an independent sovereign.
  • Between 11-14% of Thai people are considered ethnically Chinese.
  • 92.5% of Thailand’s population is Buddhist.
  • The only Southeast Asian country to never have been colonized by European forces.
